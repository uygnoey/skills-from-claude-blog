# Verifiable AI for financial research (summary guide)

## What is this guide?
A practical summary of the Kepler pattern: Claude for interpretation and ambiguity handling, paired with deterministic infrastructure for verified retrieval, computation, provenance, and audit logging.

## When is it useful?
When you are building AI for regulated environments where every figure must be auditable and traceable back to source documents.

## Key points
- Separate probabilistic reasoning from deterministic execution for correctness.
- Design every output to carry provenance (document → page → line item).
- Decompose workflows into stages and match models to each stage.
- Maintain reliability via structured domain context and automated evaluation pipelines.
