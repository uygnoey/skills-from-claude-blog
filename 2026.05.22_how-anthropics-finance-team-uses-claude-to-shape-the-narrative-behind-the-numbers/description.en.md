## What is this post?
This post explains how Anthropic’s corporate finance and strategy team uses Claude to keep a single coherent narrative behind rapidly changing financial numbers, especially for board materials.

## When is it useful?
Use this approach when you repeatedly ship finance narratives (board decks, monthly reviews) that must stay consistent even as inputs refresh up to the last minute and multiple stakeholders update in parallel.

## Key points
- Finance work is often narrative work: explaining metric movement, setting expectations, and connecting results to strategy.
- Claude can serve as an “integrity layer” that continuously checks whether claims and commentary still reconcile to the latest source-of-truth numbers.
- Two recurring workflows highlighted:
  - Board deck: reconcile every number/claim and review the story like a board member.
  - Monthly review: draft variance commentary in an established voice, then edit.
- Separate projects (and project memory) per audience help preserve tone and conventions.
