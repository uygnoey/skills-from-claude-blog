# Meet the winners of our Claude Opus 4.8 Build Day hackathon

## What is this post?

A recap of the Claude Opus 4.8 Build Day hackathon and the three winning demos (Tekton, Sim Francisco, and Custom Universe).

## When is it useful?

Useful if you want concrete examples of how teams used Claude Opus 4.8 to build end-to-end prototypes quickly, plus verification and cost/latency optimization patterns that emerged under hackathon constraints.

## Key points

- Tekton reconstructed historical buildings in 3D with an evidence chain and verification loops until tests passed.
- Sim Francisco modeled a city with 10,000 synthetic residents, then clustered them into ~300 representative personas to cut inference cost 10–100× while keeping accuracy.
- Custom Universe used Claude to choose tools/models and integrate unfamiliar tech (e.g., RealityKit) into a photo-to-3D scene pipeline.
