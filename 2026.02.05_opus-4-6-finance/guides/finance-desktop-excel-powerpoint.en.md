# Finance workflows with Claude across files, Excel, and PowerPoint

## What this covers
A practical overview of the workflows described in the post: using Claude Opus 4.6 to produce more structured first drafts for finance work, especially when your work happens in desktop files, spreadsheets, and slide decks.

## When to use this approach
- You want to keep analysis close to the source artifacts (folders, Excel workbooks, PowerPoint decks).
- You want to run multiple analyses in parallel while maintaining clear task boundaries.
- You want first-pass deliverables that are already formatted for typical finance outputs.

## Key workflow ideas
### 1) Keep work in the native artifact
- Prefer editing the workbook/deck directly rather than copying tables and charts between tools.

### 2) Ask for assumptions and structure up front
- Before computing or drafting, clarify assumptions and required outputs.

### 3) Review and validate
- Human review remains essential for high-stakes decisions.
- Validate numbers, labels, and sources before sharing externally.

### 4) Use plugins where they match your workflows
- In Cowork, plugins are described as bundles of skills plus connectors.
- The post highlights a corporate finance plugin for common workflows like journal entries, variance analysis, and reconciliation.
