# How our partners are putting Opus to work for cybersecurity

## What is this post?

Anthropic's May 21, 2026 update on the technology and services partners shipping defensive cybersecurity offerings on Claude Opus, following the Claude Security public beta. The post organizes partner work into three areas — continuous offensive testing at production scale, closing the find-to-fix gap, and getting AI into production with governance — and reports concrete results from Wiz, Palo Alto Networks (Unit 42), CrowdStrike, Accenture (Cyber.AI), TrendAI Vision One, Deloitte (CTEM on Deloitte Ascend), and PwC (Claude Native Cybersecurity). BCG, Infosys, and SentinelOne are named as also building on Opus.

## When is it useful?

- When a security buyer is comparing the live Opus-powered defensive offerings and needs a structured catalog instead of a marketing tour.
- When a CISO is mapping which partner offering matches an internal need: continuous pentesting, exposure-to-fix consolidation, or governed AI rollout.
- When a security platform/services partner is benchmarking their own offering against what is now publicly described.
- When packaging quantitative talking points for a board-ready memo (150k production assets per week pentested, a year of pentest effort in under 3 weeks, 10% → 80% coverage, 3–5 day scans → under an hour, virtual patching up to 96 days before vendor patch).

## Key points

- **Three categories of partner work** the post names: (1) continuous offensive testing at production scale, (2) closing the gap between finding and fixing vulnerabilities, (3) getting AI into production, governed.
- **Wiz Red Agent** — Opus-driven attacker reasoning across web apps and APIs, continuously running across 150,000+ production assets a week and surfacing thousands of validated high/critical findings weekly with zero false positives in customer production.
- **Palo Alto Networks Unit 42 Frontier AI Defense** — expert-led exposure analysis plus hardening roadmap; internal testing claim: a year of pentest effort in under three weeks.
- **CrowdStrike Frontier AI Readiness and Resilience Service** — combines Opus with CrowdStrike's AI Red Team Services and proprietary agent frameworks; targets the platform trusted by more than 60% of the Fortune 500.
- **Accenture Cyber.AI** — Opus-powered agentic loop spanning detection, prioritization, and remediation; on Accenture's own infrastructure took coverage from ~10% to 80%+ across 1,600 applications and 500,000+ APIs and cut scans from 3–5 days to under an hour.
- **TrendAI Vision One** — Opus-assisted research feeding virtual patching across 185 countries, with disclosure into the TrendAI Zero Day Initiative protecting at-risk systems up to 96 days before a vendor patch.
- **Deloitte CTEM on Deloitte Ascend** — discovery → validation → prioritization → remediation in one workflow, including countermeasure design when no patch exists; Opus reasoning over code and automated stability testing shortens remediation from days/weeks to hours.
- **PwC Claude Native Cybersecurity** — Secure AI Adoption (sandbox-to-production in weeks) plus Scaled Frontier Defense (Opus reasoning inside existing vuln management, detection, security engineering, and GRC, with guardrails and auditability).
- **Growing ecosystem** — BCG, Infosys, and SentinelOne are also building on Opus; more details to follow.
- **Common substrate** — every offering above shares the same Opus capabilities: reasoning over code, mapping exposures to real-world risk, and sustaining long agentic workflows.
